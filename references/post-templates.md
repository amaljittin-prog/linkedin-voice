# Full Post Templates — With Annotations

## HOW TO USE
These are NOT copy-paste templates. They are SHAPES.
Replace every [bracket] with something specific to this person.
Specific > General. Always.

---

## TEMPLATE 1: THE FAILURE STORY
*Best for: Maximum comments. Highest emotional resonance.*
*Trigger: Vulnerability + Curiosity Gap*

```
[Single word or short shock line that sets the scene.]

[2 lines: What you thought was happening / What you believed]

[2 lines: The moment it went wrong. Be specific — where were you, what happened]

[1 line: Your immediate reaction. The honest emotion.]

[2 lines: What you did after. The messy middle.]

[2 lines: What you now understand that you didn't before]

[1 line: The real lesson — stated simply, not cleverly]

[Specific question: "Has this ever happened to you?" or "What would you have done?"]

#hashtag1 #hashtag2 #hashtag3
```

**Annotated Example:**
```
Rejected.

I applied for what I thought was a perfect internship.
Prepared for 2 weeks. Wrote what I thought was a strong application.

The rejection email came in 6 minutes.
6 minutes. They didn't even read it.

I was furious. Then embarrassed. Then genuinely confused.

I spent the next 3 days figuring out what went wrong.
Turns out I had addressed the email to the wrong company name.
I had copy-pasted from another application.

The lesson I actually learned:
Slow down before you send. Speed is not the same as urgency.

What's the most embarrassing mistake you made in your career? 
I'll go first — now it's your turn.

#CareerLessons #Internship #BBAStudent
```

## TEMPLATE 2: THE INSIGHT POST
*Best for: Saves + shares. Building authority and credibility.*
*Trigger: Social Proof + Curiosity Gap*

```
[Bold claim — state the insight directly. Don't bury it.]

[2 lines: Why most people get this wrong / what they do instead]

[3-4 lines: The actual insight broken into simple steps or observations]
→ [Point 1]
→ [Point 2]
→ [Point 3]

[1-2 lines: The honest nuance or caveat]

[Lead magnet line OR direct question]

#hashtag1 #hashtag2 #hashtag3
```

**Annotated Example:**
```
Most Indian students prepare for interviews wrong.

They memorize answers.
Companies are looking for thinking.

Here's what actually works:

→ Study the company's real problems, not just their "about us" page
→ Prepare 3 questions that show you understand their business
→ Practice talking about a mistake you made — most people can't do this honestly
→ Research the person interviewing you (LinkedIn takes 5 minutes)

None of this is in any placement preparation guide.
All of it will make you memorable.

Save this before your next interview. 🔖

What's one interview tip that actually worked for you?

#PlacementSeason #InterviewTips #IndianStudents
```

## TEMPLATE 3: THE OBSERVATION POST
*Best for: Building unique voice. Shows you think differently.*
*Trigger: Pattern Interrupt + Curiosity Gap*

```
[Observation stated as fact — something specific you noticed]

[2 lines: The context — where were you, what were you doing]

[3 lines: What you observed in detail — the specific, concrete thing]

[2 lines: What this made you think / the question it raised]

[1-2 lines: Your conclusion or what you changed because of this]

[Question that invites others to share their observations]

#hashtag1 #hashtag2 #hashtag3
```

**Annotated Example:**
```
The chai tapri near my college does ₹12,000 a day.
No app. No advertising. No business degree.

He's been there 11 years.
Same spot, same price, same faces every morning.

What he actually has:
→ Zero customer acquisition cost (location is everything)
→ 100% repeat customers (product-market fit is perfect)
→ No rent, no employees, no inventory waste
→ Profit margins most startups would kill for

Every MBA framework I've studied — he's living it.
Without ever opening a textbook.

The best business education I got this semester
was buying chai for ₹15.

Who's the most unexpected business lesson you've learned?

#BusinessLessons #Startup #IndianBusiness
```

## TEMPLATE 4: THE CONTROVERSIAL TAKE
*Best for: Comments, debate, new followers with strong opinions.*
*Trigger: Pattern Interrupt + Loss Aversion*

```
[Hot take — state the unpopular opinion directly. No softening.]

[1 line: Acknowledge why people disagree]

[3 lines: Your actual evidence or reasoning]

[1 line: The nuance — what you're NOT saying]

[Direct invitation to debate]

#hashtag1 #hashtag2 #hashtag3
```

**Annotated Example:**
```
A BBA from a tier-2 college in India is worth more than an MBA from a bad tier-1 college.

I know that's not what everyone wants to hear.

But here's what I've observed:
→ Skills learned on the ground beat credentials earned by rote
→ ₹25 lakh in loan debt follows you for 8 years
→ The network you build matters more than the network they promise you

I'm not saying don't do MBA.
I'm saying: know exactly WHY you're doing it.

Agree or disagree? Tell me why. I want to be wrong about this.

#MBA #BBA #IndianEducation
```
