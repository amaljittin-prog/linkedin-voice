# Hooks Library — 60+ Hooks Organized by Psychological Trigger

## HOW TO USE THIS FILE
Pick the trigger that matches the post goal.
Never copy a hook word-for-word — use it as a SHAPE, not a script.
The best hook is always one that only THIS person could write.

---

## CURIOSITY GAP HOOKS
*(Open a loop. Force them to read to close it.)*

**Personal mystery:**
- "Something happened last week that I haven't been able to stop thinking about."
- "I made a decision last month that everyone around me called stupid."
- "I said something in a meeting that made the whole room go quiet."
- "I got rejected last year in a way I didn't expect."
- "I found out something about my industry that genuinely shocked me."

**Tease without revealing:**
- "There are two types of people in every interview. Most people don't know which one they are."
- "I asked 10 professionals the same question. 9 gave the same wrong answer."
- "The thing that's holding most Indian students back isn't what anyone talks about."
- "After 3 months of [X], I learned something I wish I'd known on day 1."

**Numbers without context:**
- "47. That's how many times I was told no before yes."
- "₹0. That's what my first 'project' made. Here's what I learned."
- "3 AM. That's when I figured out what I actually wanted to do with my career."

## PATTERN INTERRUPT HOOKS
*(Break their prediction. Snap attention on.)*

**Single powerful word:**
- "Fired."
- "Rejected."  
- "Wrong."
- "Stop."
- "Wait."

**Contrarian statement:**
- "Your GPA doesn't matter as much as your college tells you."
- "Most internship advice you've heard is backwards."
- "Working harder is not the answer. (I say this as someone who worked harder for 2 years.)"
- "LinkedIn posts about 'grinding' are the worst thing for your career."
- "The students who struggle the most in placements are usually the most prepared."

**Unexpected admission:**
- "I used to be the worst person in my team. I'm not embarrassed to say it anymore."
- "I gave terrible advice to a junior last year. I think about it regularly."
- "I almost quit [X] twice. Here's what stopped me — and whether it was right."

## LOSS AVERSION / FOMO HOOKS
*(Fear of missing out is 2x more powerful than hope)*

**Warning frame:**
- "If you're a student in India right now, there's something important you need to understand."
- "Most people making this mistake don't realize it until it's already cost them 2 years."
- "I wish someone had told me this before my first semester."
- "The career advice everyone gives Indian freshers is quietly setting them up to struggle."

**Scarcity of knowledge:**
- "Almost nobody in [field] talks about this publicly."
- "This is the thing successful people in India rarely post about."
- "What most LinkedIn posts won't tell you about [topic]:"

## VULNERABILITY / AUTHENTICITY HOOKS
*(Admission of failure or confusion creates deepest connection)*

**Raw confession:**
- "I had a LinkedIn account for months and never posted once. I was scared."
- "I failed my first internship interview. Here's exactly what happened."
- "I cried in a bathroom stall during my first week at work. Not my proudest moment."
- "I genuinely didn't know what I was doing for the first 6 months of my [role/course]."

**Honest doubt:**
- "I'm not sure this is the right thing to share. But I think it needs to be said."
- "I might be wrong about this. But here's what I've observed."
- "This is something I've been hesitant to post about. But here goes."

**Before the lesson:**
- "Two years ago, I thought [wrong belief]. I was completely wrong."
- "I used to judge people who [X]. Then it happened to me."
- "The version of me from 1 year ago would disagree with everything in this post."

---

## SOCIAL PROOF + BELONGING HOOKS
*(People trust what others like them do)*

**Collective observation:**
- "Every person I've talked to who [achieved X] did this one thing differently."
- "I've interviewed [X] people about this. The pattern is impossible to ignore."
- "3 different seniors gave me the same advice. I ignored it. I shouldn't have."

**Invitation to belong:**
- "If you've ever felt like you're the only one struggling with [X], read this."
- "This is for the students from tier-2 cities who feel like they're playing a different game."
- "Every fresher in India needs to understand this before their first job."

---

## TRANSFORMATION HOOKS
*(Before → After. Neurologically irresistible)*

**Specific transformation:**
- "6 months ago I couldn't get a callback. This month I had 3 offers."
- "I went from knowing nobody in [industry] to having conversations with people I admired. Here's exactly how."
- "From ₹0 to my first paid project. The part nobody talks about is what happened in between."

**Mindset shift:**
- "The way I think about [X] completely changed after one conversation."
- "I had a belief about [X] that I held for years. It was wrong."
- "One question changed how I approach everything in my career."

## INDIA-SPECIFIC HOOKS
*(For the Indian professional/student context)*

**City/background:**
- "I'm from [small city]. Nobody in my family had worked in a corporate office before me."
- "Growing up in [city], I never saw someone who looked like me doing [X]."
- "Tier-2 city. Average grades. No connections. Here's how I [achieved X]."

**Relatable Indian moments:**
- "My parents wanted me to be a CA. I chose [X]. Here's what happened."
- "Placement season hit different this year. Here's what I actually saw."
- "The chai tapri near my campus taught me more about business than any lecture."
- "I asked my uncle for career advice. He gave me ₹500 instead. He was right."

**Education reality:**
- "My BBA taught me a lot. None of it was what I actually needed to know."
- "The gap between what colleges teach and what companies want is wider than anyone admits."
- "Campus placement offices are optimizing for [X]. Here's what you actually need."
